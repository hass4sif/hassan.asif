// 01   identity

Hassan Asif. حسن آصف

Scholar of digital religion, remix, and infrastructure.

I study how AI assisted edits, captions, and audio travel across platforms and reshape doctrine, authority, and aesthetic form. My work combines ethnography, close reading of audiovisual remixes, and media genealogy, with sustained attention to platforms, archives, and informal media economies. Fieldwork to date has centred on Pakistan and its global diasporas.

// 02 now

MAY 2026

I defended my PhD in Information at the University of Toronto in May 2026, with a collaborative specialization in South Asian Studies. Conferral is in October. The dissertation, Remixing Devotion: Digital Media Practices in Pakistan, follows na'at and Islamic sermon media through home studios, repair markets, broadcast control rooms, and WhatsApp chains.

I am now turning chapters into articles and a book proposal, co-editing a volume on Digital Religion with Hassan Bashir (submission Winter 2026), and teaching at UTM ICCIT.

Recent recognition: Chancellor Jackman Graduate Fellow, Margaret Higgins Memorial Fellow, Amilcare Iannucci Graduate Fellow in the Humanities, SSHRC Doctoral Scholar.
