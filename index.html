Peer-reviewed journal articles

2025article
What is it about?
Discostan and Hamnawa: Between erasure and preservation in South Asian digital diasporic archives
Media, Culture and Society, 47(8), 1599 to 1618.
How two small online archives keep South Asian music alive when the big platforms forget it. One is Discostan, a Los Angeles record label. The other is Hamnawa, a publication on Pakistani pop.
2024article
What is it about?
"Blur the preacher": Media remix and digital technocultural practice in Pakistan
Journal of Religion, Media and Digital Culture, 13(3), 363 to 386.
YouTube remixers in Pakistan cut, blur, and rebuild religious sermons. The article asks what happens to a preacher's authority when his words get edited and reshared.
in pressarticle
What is it about?
Obfuscation as revelation: Knowledge sharing, piracy, and Islamic devotional remixing in Pakistan
Digital Humanities Quarterly. Special issue on the Global South.
Pakistani religious music producers often work with pirated software. The article argues that the cracked tools are part of how the craft is taught and shared, not a problem to be cleaned up.
Dec 2026forthcoming
What is it about?
Moral traffic: How WhatsApp moves media, rules, and resources in Pakistan's devotional media ecologies
Mobile Media and Communication. Special issue on messaging applications.
WhatsApp groups in Pakistan move audio files, religious rulings, and donations through the same channels. The article treats the group chat as a piece of real infrastructure.
under reviewarticle
What is it about?
IslaMixing: Hauntology as an experimental framework for exploring online #Islamogram memes
Journal of Media and Religion.
An experimental piece on Islamic memes online. It uses ideas about how the past haunts the present to read what circulates under the #Islamogram tag.

In D. Sundaram and C. Tekobbe (Eds.), Social Media, Religion, and Culture. Routledge.

Technical imagination against deepfakes: Reading, bending, and exposing the image game
